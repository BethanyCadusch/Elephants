What is the African Forest Elephant?

The African Forest Elephant's scientific name is Loxodonta cyclotis. African Forest elephants are the smallest out of the two African species. They live in central and southern Africa among forest, savannah, and flood plains.

Survival

The African Forest elephant is listed as endangered due to humans poaching for bushmeat and ivory (which is the white material from their tusks). But humans are not the only predators that stand in its way, lions and hyenas also hunt on the Forest elephant. All elephants are hebivores and the African Forest elephant feeds on grass, fruit, and roots throughtout the day.
